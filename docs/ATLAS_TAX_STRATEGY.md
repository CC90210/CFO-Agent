## CRYPTO TAX SPECIFICS

### ACB (Adjusted Cost Base) Tracking

**Canada requires the weighted average method:**
```
ACB per unit = Total cost of all units acquired / Total number of units held
```

**Include in cost:**
- Purchase price
- Exchange fees (buying and selling)
- Gas/network fees
- Transfer fees

**Tools for ACB calculation:**
1. **Koinly** — Import Kraken CSV, auto-calculates ACB, generates CRA-ready reports
2. **CoinTracker** — Similar, good Kraken integration
3. **AdjustedCostBase.ca** — Free, manual entry
4. **CryptoTaxCalculator** — Canadian-specific

**Action:** Export full 2024 transaction history from Kraken → Import to Koinly → Generate tax report

### Superficial Loss Rule

- Sell crypto at a loss + repurchase **same crypto** within 30 days before or after = **loss denied**
- Denied loss added to ACB of repurchased units
- 61-day window total (30 before + sale day + 30 after)
- **Workaround:** Sell BTC at a loss, buy ETH immediately (different crypto = no superficial loss)
- **Or:** Wait 31 days before rebuying same crypto

### Tax-Loss Harvesting Strategy

**Before December 31 each year:**
1. Review portfolio for unrealized losses
2. Sell losing positions to crystallize losses
3. Offset against gains (capital gains) or reduce business income
4. Wait 31 days before rebuying same crypto (or buy different crypto immediately)
5. **ATLAS can automate this** — flag positions with unrealized losses in Q4

### Crypto-to-Crypto Swaps

Every swap is a taxable disposition:
- Trading BTC → ETH = deemed sale of BTC at fair market value
- Must calculate gain/loss on the BTC based on ACB
- The ETH acquisition cost = fair market value at time of swap

## ADVANCED TAX STRATEGIES (Elite Accountant Knowledge)

These are the strategies that $500+/hour CPAs deploy for their clients. Every one is legal
under the Income Tax Act — the edge comes from knowing they exist and timing them correctly.

### Strategy 1: GST/HST Voluntary Registration (ITC Recovery)

**ITA Reference:** Excise Tax Act, Part IX

If OASIS revenue is under $30,000 (the mandatory registration threshold), **voluntarily register
for HST anyway**. Why:

- You charge clients 13% HST on invoices (they claim it back as ITC — net zero to them)
- You claim **Input Tax Credits (ITCs)** on ALL business purchases: computer, software, internet, phone, hosting
- On the first few returns, your ITCs may exceed HST collected = **CRA sends you a refund**
- Net effect: CRA pays YOU back 13% of your business expenses

**Example:** $5,000 in business expenses × 13% = $650 refund from CRA (if you collected less HST than ITCs)

**Filing:** HST return (quarterly or annually) via CRA My Business Account. Simple form.

**When NOT to do this:** If all your clients are individuals (not businesses) who can't claim ITCs,
the 13% price increase may cost you clients. Since OASIS serves businesses, they claim ITCs — this is free money.

### Strategy 2: FHSA Contribution Deduction Timing (Carry Forward the Deduction)

**ITA Reference:** Section 146.6

Unlike RRSP, FHSA contributions made in the first 60 days of a year **cannot** be deducted
against the prior year. However, you CAN carry forward the deduction indefinitely.

**Strategy:** Contribute $8,000 to FHSA now. If your income is low this year but you expect
higher income in future years, **don't claim the deduction this year**. Carry it forward and
deduct it in a year when your marginal rate is 29.65%+ instead of 20.05%.

**Math:**
- $8K deduction at 20.05% marginal rate = $1,604 saved
- $8K deduction at 29.65% marginal rate = $2,372 saved
- **Delta: $768 more** by waiting to claim the deduction

**Rule:** Always contribute NOW (to get tax-free growth started), but time the DEDUCTION for
highest-income years.

### Strategy 3: Medical Expense Tax Credit (METC) 12-Month Timing

**ITA Reference:** Section 118.2

The METC allows you to claim expenses from **any 12-month period ending in the tax year**.
This is NOT limited to the calendar year.

**Strategy:** Bundle 15-16 months of medical expenses into one claim:
- Claim period: Feb 1, 2023 to Jan 31, 2024 (for 2024 tax year)
- This lets you pull in expenses from the previous year that you didn't claim

**Self-employed health premium deduction (better than METC):**
If you pay for private health/dental insurance, deduct premiums on T2125 as a business expense
instead of claiming METC. Business deduction saves at your marginal rate (~20-30%); METC only
saves 15% federal + 5.05% Ontario = 20.05%.

**For future (incorporated):** Set up a Health Spending Account (HSA) through the corporation.
100% of medical expenses become before-tax business deductions. This is how incorporated
professionals legally get tax-free healthcare.

### Strategy 4: Capital Gains Reserve (Section 40 — Spread Gains Over 5 Years)

**ITA Reference:** ITA s. 40(1)(a)(iii)

If you sell an asset (business, property, large crypto position) on an installment basis
(seller financing), you can spread the capital gain over up to 5 years.

**Formula:** Minimum 20% of gain recognized per year.

**How it works:**
```
Year 1: Report proceeds received / total proceeds × capital gain (minimum 20%)
Year 2: Report cumulative minimum 40%
Year 3: Report cumulative minimum 60%
Year 4: Report cumulative minimum 80%
Year 5: Report remaining 100%
```

**Why this matters:** If you sell OASIS for $500K someday, instead of a $500K capital gain
hitting one tax year (potentially at 66.67% inclusion above $250K), you spread it over 5
years and keep more below the $250K threshold each year.

**Requirements:** Proceeds must genuinely not be received upfront. Structure as a promissory note.

### Strategy 5: Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption (LCGE) — $1.25M Tax-Free

**ITA Reference:** Section 110.6

**Updated 2024:** The LCGE is now **$1,250,000** (increased from $1,016,836), effective June 25, 2024.
Annual indexation resumes in 2026.

**What this means for OASIS:**
- If you incorporate OASIS and later sell the qualified small business corporation (QSBC) shares
- The first **$1.25 million** in capital gains is **completely tax-free**
- At 50% inclusion and 53.53% top rate, this saves up to **$334,375 in tax**

**Qualification requirements (must meet ALL):**
1. Corporation must be a CCPC at time of sale
2. >90% of assets must be used in active Canadian business at time of sale
3. >50% of assets used in active business throughout the 24 months before sale
4. Shares must be held for at least 24 months
5. Nobody other than you or a related person owned the shares in the last 24 months

**Planning:** When you incorporate OASIS, keep it clean — no passive investments inside the corp
until you've used the LCGE. Hold investments personally or in a separate holding company.

## CROSS-BORDER TAX STRATEGY (US Bank Accounts + International)

CC holds bank accounts in both Canada and the United States. This creates specific
reporting obligations and tax optimization opportunities.

### T1135 — Foreign Income Verification Statement (CRITICAL)

**Threshold:** If your specified foreign property (including US bank accounts, US investments,
foreign crypto exchanges) exceeds **$100,000 CAD total cost at ANY point during the year**,
you MUST file Form T1135.

**What counts as specified foreign property:**
- US bank accounts (chequing, savings, brokerage)
- Foreign crypto exchange balances (Kraken is a Canadian exchange — check if funds are held in US entity)
- US stocks held outside a Canadian brokerage
- Foreign real estate (not personal vacation home)
- Foreign business assets

**What does NOT count:**
- USD accounts held at a **Canadian bank** (RBC USD account, TD USD account — these are domestic)
- Property used exclusively in active business
- Personal-use property (vacation home under certain conditions)

**Penalties for not filing T1135:**
- $25/day, max $2,500 (basic)
- $1,000/month up to $24,000 if CRA demands and you don't comply
- **Extended reassessment period** — CRA gets 3 extra years to audit you

**Action:** Determine if CC's US bank account balance + other foreign property > $100K CAD.
If yes, file T1135 with your T1. If no, document that it was below threshold.

### US Bank Account — Tax Treatment

**Key rule:** Canada taxes **worldwide income**. Interest earned in US bank accounts must
be reported as foreign income on your Canadian T1, even if it stays in the US account.

**US withholding tax on interest:**
- Under the Canada-US Tax Treaty, interest is generally exempt from US withholding
- If any US tax is withheld, claim a **Foreign Tax Credit** (FTC) on your Canadian return (Form T2209)
- The FTC prevents double taxation — dollar-for-dollar offset

**Currency conversion:**
- Report all US income in CAD using the Bank of Canada exchange rate on the date received
- OR use the annual average rate for convenience (CRA accepts either method, be consistent)

### Wyoming / Delaware LLC Strategy (Advanced — Future Use)

**For digital businesses like OASIS that serve international clients:**

A **Wyoming LLC** offers significant advantages for non-US-resident owners:
- $0 state income tax (Wyoming has none)
- $60/year annual report fee (cheapest in the US)
- Single-member LLC owned by a non-US-resident = **no US federal tax** on non-US-sourced income
- Strong privacy protections (no public ownership disclosure)
- FinCEN BOI report required within 30 days of formation (2025+)

**BUT — THE CRITICAL CANADA TAX TRAP:**

CRA treats a US LLC as a **corporation**, not a pass-through. This means:
1. The LLC's income is NOT automatically your personal income for Canadian tax purposes
2. If the LLC earns passive income → **FAPI rules** apply (Foreign Accrual Property Income)
3. CRA will tax you on the LLC's passive income as if you received it, even if you didn't withdraw it
4. You lose the small business deduction on LLC income (it's foreign, not Canadian active business)

**When a US LLC DOES make sense for CC:**
- Receiving payments from US clients (USD invoicing, US payment processors)
- Opening a US Stripe/payment account (requires US entity)
- Building a US business presence for future expansion
- **NOT for tax reduction** — Canada taxes worldwide income regardless

**Better structure for tax optimization:**
```
Canadian CCPC (OASIS AI Solutions Inc.)
  └── Optional: US C-Corp subsidiary (if US operations grow)
       └── Treaty-rate dividends back to Canadian parent (5% withholding vs 30%)
```

**Bottom line:** A Wyoming LLC is useful for **operational reasons** (US banking, US payment
processing, US clients). It is NOT a tax haven for Canadian residents — CRA's FAPI rules
and worldwide taxation close that door.

### Offshore Structures — Reality Check for Canadian Residents

**What the ultra-wealthy actually do (and CRA's countermeasures):**

| Structure | How it works | CRA Countermeasure |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Cayman Islands holding co | 0% corporate tax, hold investments | FAPI rules tax passive income in Canadian's hands |
| Barbados CCPC | 2.5% tax rate, treaty access | CRA added specific anti-avoidance rules for Barbados |
| Panama foundation | Privacy, asset protection | T1135 reporting required; GAAR if no business substance |
| Singapore holding co | Territorial tax (0% on foreign income) | FAPI + controlled foreign affiliate rules |

**The honest truth:** For someone at CC's income level ($49K-$100K CAD), offshore structures
cost more in compliance ($5K-$15K/year in legal and accounting fees) than they save. The
ROI only makes sense above **$500K+ annual income** where the tax deferral exceeds
compliance costs.

**What DOES work at CC's level:**
1. FHSA + TFSA + RRSP = $0 tax on growth (legally, domestically, zero compliance cost)
2. Incorporation (CCPC) at $80K+ = 12.2% corporate rate with tax deferral
3. LCGE on OASIS sale = $1.25M tax-free
4. US LLC for **operational purposes** only (payment processing, US clients)
