## 3. UK FIG Regime — The Gateway Strategy

### 3.1 Background — What Replaced Non-Dom

The UK non-domicile remittance basis regime, which had operated for over 200 years, was abolished effective **6 April 2025** under the Finance Act 2025. It has been replaced by the Foreign Income and Gains (FIG) regime — a residence-based system, not domicile-based.

This is an improvement for CC: the old remittance basis required careful management of offshore accounts to avoid "remitting" income to the UK, triggering tax. Under FIG, there is **no remittance requirement** — CC can bring all foreign income into UK bank accounts, spend it freely on UK property or investments, with no UK income tax for four years.

**Authority:** Finance Act 2025, Part 2, Sections 13-27; HMRC Guidance "Check if you can claim the 4-year Foreign Income and Gains regime" (GOV.UK, 2025)

### 3.2 FIG Eligibility — CC Qualifies Perfectly

| Condition | Requirement | CC's Status |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| UK tax residence | Must be UK tax resident | Achievable by moving to England/Scotland/Wales |
| Prior non-residence | Must NOT have been UK resident in any of the 10 tax years before the claim year | CC has NEVER lived in UK — satisfies easily |
| Claim election | Must elect on Self Assessment return each year | Elect on first UK return |
| Duration | 4 consecutive tax years maximum | Full 4 years available |

CC has never been UK tax resident. This means the full 4-year FIG window is open from the moment CC moves to the UK.

### 3.3 What FIG Covers — 0% on Foreign Income and Gains

During the FIG period, CC pays **zero UK income tax** on:

- SaaS revenue from Canadian and global clients (foreign-source business income)
- Crypto capital gains on assets held outside UK
- Dividends from non-UK companies
- Interest from non-UK bank accounts
- Royalties from foreign IP licensing
- Capital gains on foreign assets (shares, crypto, business interests)

**Note on "foreign source":** OASIS AI Solutions revenue is foreign-source income because the clients are Canadian/global, the business is structured outside the UK, and the income arises outside the UK. Cloud-hosted SaaS with no UK physical infrastructure = foreign-source confirmed.

### 3.5 The UK-to-IoM Pipeline — Why FIG Is Phase 2, Not the End State

FIG lasts exactly 4 years. After Year 4, all foreign income becomes taxable in the UK at normal rates (20-45%). The strategy is to use FIG as a **bridge** while establishing IoM residency and company:

```
Year 0:   Depart Canada (departure tax filed, T1244 elected if needed)
Years 1-4: UK FIG — 0% on foreign SaaS income, NI only ~£3,500-5,000/year
Year 4:   Leave UK — UK has NO exit tax on individuals
Year 5+:  Isle of Man residence — 0% corporate, ~16% effective personal, 0% CGT
```

**UK Exit Tax Risk:** None. Unlike Canada (s.128.1) and the US (Section 877A), the UK does not impose an exit tax on individuals. CC can leave the UK with no deemed disposition event.

**IHT Protection:** CC will be UK resident for only 4 years. Long-Term UK Resident status for IHT purposes requires 10 out of 20 years residence (Finance Act 2025, s.58 — effective April 2025, replacing old 15-year domicile test). 4 years is well below the threshold — CC leaves UK IHT-free.

### 3.6 Establishing UK Residence — Statutory Residence Test

CC must be UK tax resident to claim FIG. The Statutory Residence Test (SRT) applies:

**Automatic UK Residence** (most straightforward for CC):
- Spend 183+ days in UK in a tax year, OR
- Have only home in UK (and spend any time there)

CC's recommended approach: move to UK, sign a lease, spend the majority of time there. Day-count threshold of 183 days is a safe harbor but not the only route.

**CRITICAL:** Must live in England, Scotland, or Wales. The Isle of Man and Channel Islands are NOT part of the UK for tax purposes — they are separate jurisdictions. Moving directly to IoM bypasses the FIG window entirely.

## 11. Canada Departure Tax and Treaty Analysis

### 11.1 Departure Tax — Deemed Disposition (ITA s.128.1)

**Authority:** Income Tax Act (Canada) s.128.1(4); CRA IT-451R; CRA T4056 (Emigrants and Income Tax)

When CC ceases Canadian residency, ITA s.128.1(4) triggers a deemed disposition of all worldwide property at Fair Market Value on the date of departure. This creates a taxable event on unrealized gains — CC must calculate and pay tax as if all property was sold on departure day.

**Properties subject to deemed disposition:**
- Cryptocurrency holdings (current FMV - ACB = taxable gain)
- Investment portfolio (shares, ETFs, bonds)
- Business goodwill and intellectual property (OASIS AI Solutions brand, IP)
- Foreign real estate (if any)
- Shares in private corporations (including CCPC if incorporated)

**Properties EXEMPT from deemed disposition:**
- Canadian real estate (taxed when actually sold, under s.116)
- RRSPs and RRIFs (taxed when funds withdrawn, s.146(1))
- TFSAs (contributions/gains remain exempt — no deemed disposition)
- FHSA (no deemed disposition — confirmed by CRA)
- CPP and OAS entitlements
- Employee stock options (treated separately)

### 11.2 Departure Tax Mitigation Strategies

| Strategy | Mechanism | Saving Potential |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **T1244 Election — Deferral** | File election with T1 return; defer payment until actual disposition; security required if tax >$16,500 | Cash flow — no payment until assets actually sold |
| **Maximize TFSA before departure** | Move appreciated assets into TFSA (via in-kind contribution or sell/rebuy); no deemed disposition on TFSA | Shields future gains from all tax, not just departure tax |
| **Realize losses before departure** | Sell underwater positions before departure date; loss offsets departure tax gains | Dollar-for-dollar offset against gains |
| **Superficial loss rule watch (ITA s.54)** | Cannot repurchase identical property within 30 days before/after — must be clean break | Avoid triggering superficial loss denial |
| **FHSA opening** | Sheltered from deemed disposition; opens $8K/year deduction while resident | $1,600-2,400 deduction value per year |
| **Hold crypto in TFSA via ETFs** | Horizon Bitcoin ETF (BTCX), Fidelity Advantage Bitcoin ETF (FBTC.U) — grow tax-free, no deemed dispo | Potentially large (crypto has high appreciation) |
| **IP valuation strategy** | Commission independent IP valuation; low business goodwill value = low deemed disposition | Directly reduces departure tax on IP |

### 11.3 T1244 Election — Deferral Details

**Authority:** ITA s.220(4.5); T1244 Form (Election to Defer Payment of Tax on Income Relating to the Deemed Disposition of Property)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Filing deadline | April 30 of the year following departure |
| Applies to | All property subject to deemed disposition |
| Security threshold | Required if departure tax exceeds **$16,500** |
| Acceptable security | Letter of credit, GIC, charge on property |
| When tax becomes due | On actual disposition of property (sale) |
| Partial coverage | Can elect on some properties and pay immediately on others |

**Recommendation:** If CC has significant crypto or IP gains at departure, T1244 election is essential. Preserves working capital for the move while deferring the tax liability until the actual monetization event.

### 11.4 Canada-UK Treaty — Residence Tie-Breaker

**Authority:** Canada-United Kingdom Income Tax Convention (1978), Article 4; Protocol (2014)

When CC ceases Canadian residency and becomes UK resident under FIG, the treaty governs which country has primary taxing rights during any overlap period. Article 4 tie-breaker rules apply sequentially:

| Priority | Tie-Breaker Rule | CC's Situation |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Permanent home (where CC has permanent dwelling) | UK lease signed = UK permanent home |
| 2 | Centre of vital interests (personal and economic relations) | UK: new business base, bank accounts, social life |
| 3 | Habitual abode (where CC spends more time) | UK: physically present |
| 4 | Nationality | Both Canadian and British — TIE |
| 5 | Mutual agreement | Competent authorities negotiate |

With a clean break from Canada (no retained lease, no family obligations), tie-breakers 1-3 resolve to UK. UK residency confirmed for treaty purposes.

### 11.5 Article 7 — No Canadian PE Risk

**Authority:** Canada-UK Treaty, Article 7 (Business Profits)

SaaS business profits are only taxable in Canada if attributable to a Permanent Establishment in Canada. A PE requires:
- Fixed place of business (office, server, etc.) — CC has none post-departure
- Dependent agent acting on CC's behalf — CC has no Canadian employees
- Construction project — not applicable

**Cloud-hosted SaaS with no physical Canadian infrastructure = no PE = Canada has no taxing right on business profits.**

Defensive measure: migrate hosting infrastructure to non-Canadian servers (AWS us-east-1 → EU-west-1 or IoM-hosted) before or on departure date.

A clean break is essential to establish non-residency and avoid CRA challenge. The following must be completed:
