## 2.1 Bookkeeping Automation

### Revenue Auto-Sync (Stripe → Accounting)

**Setup:**
1. Connect Stripe to Wave (free) or QBO ($25/mo) via native integration
2. Every Stripe payout auto-creates a revenue entry
3. Stripe fees auto-categorize as "Payment Processing Fees" (T2125 Line 8871)
4. Multi-currency: Stripe converts USD → CAD at payout; accounting software records CAD amount
5. Reconcile monthly — Stripe dashboard vs. accounting software (should match exactly)

**Rules:**
- Stripe gross revenue = your income (not net-of-fees)
- Stripe fees = deductible expense
- Stripe payouts to Wise = bank transfer, not revenue (avoid double-counting)

### Expense Auto-Sync (Wise → Accounting)

**Setup:**
1. Connect Wise to accounting software via API or CSV import
2. Set auto-categorization rules:
   - "Anthropic" → Software/SaaS (T2125 Line 8871)
   - "DigitalOcean" / "AWS" / "Vercel" → Hosting/Cloud (T2125 Line 8871)
   - "Serato" / "SoundCloud" → Music Subscriptions (T2125 Line 8871)
   - "Gas" / "Shell" / "Esso" → Vehicle Fuel (track business %)
3. Flag uncategorized transactions weekly (should be <20% after rules mature)
4. FX conversion: Wise records CAD equivalent; use this for bookkeeping

### Bank Feed Auto-Categorization

**Target:** 80% of transactions auto-categorized within 3 months of training the rules.

| Transaction Pattern | Auto-Category | T2125 Line |
|---------------------|---------------|------------|
| Stripe payouts | Revenue | 8299 |
| Monthly subscriptions (known vendors) | Software | 8871 |
| OANDA/Kraken deposits | Transfer (non-revenue) | N/A |
| Restaurant/coffee (flag for review) | Meals & Entertainment (50%) | 8523 |
| Gas stations | Vehicle (business %) | 9281 |
| Client name match | Revenue | 8299 |

### Receipt Capture Workflow

**Option A — HubDoc (free with QBO):**
1. Email receipts to your HubDoc inbox (auto-assigned address)
2. Photograph paper receipts with HubDoc phone app
3. HubDoc auto-extracts vendor, date, amount, HST
4. Auto-publishes to QBO as bill/expense

**Option B — Manual Cloud Folder (free, works with any software):**
1. Create Google Drive folder: `Receipts/2026/[Month]`
2. Photograph every receipt immediately (phone camera)
3. File naming convention: `2026-03-27_Vendor_$Amount.jpg`
4. During monthly close, match receipts to transactions
5. **Retention:** Keep digital copies 6 years minimum (CRA requirement)

**Option C — Dext ($20/mo):**
1. Snap receipt with Dext app → auto-extract all fields
2. Auto-publish to QBO/Xero
3. Best OCR accuracy of all options
4. Overkill at CC's current transaction volume; worth it at 100+ receipts/month

**Atlas Recommendation:** Start with Option B (free). Move to HubDoc when on QBO. Move to Dext if transaction volume exceeds 100/month.

### Monthly Reconciliation Checklist (30 minutes)

```
[ ] 1. Open accounting software — review all unrecategorized transactions
[ ] 2. Open Stripe dashboard — confirm total revenue matches accounting
[ ] 3. Open Wise statements — confirm all payouts and expenses recorded
[ ] 4. Open RBC statement — confirm no missed business transactions
[ ] 5. Open Kraken/OANDA — record any realized gains/losses
[ ] 6. Match receipt folder to expense entries — flag any missing receipts

[ ] 8. Review Accounts Receivable — any unpaid invoices > 30 days?

[ ] 10. Run P&L report — compare to prior month and annual budget
```

### Tax Provision — Auto Set-Aside

**Rule:** Transfer 25% of every business payment received to a separate "Tax Reserve" account.

**Why 25%:**
- Federal tax on self-employment income at $50K-$100K: ~15%-20.5%
- Ontario provincial: ~5%-9%
- CPP self-employment (both halves): 11.9% on pensionable earnings
- 25% covers the combined burden with a small buffer
- At higher income ($100K+), increase to 30%

**Automation:**
1. Open a dedicated RBC savings account or Wise "jar" — label it "TAX RESERVE"
2. When each Wise/Stripe payment arrives, immediately transfer 25% to Tax Reserve
3. This money is NOT yours. It belongs to CRA.
4. Use Tax Reserve to pay installments (Mar 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Dec 15)
5. Use remaining Tax Reserve to pay balance owing on April 30

**Wise automation:** Wise does not auto-transfer, but you can set a recurring monthly transfer or use the "Jars" feature to manually move 25% on payment day. Takes 30 seconds.

```
  (If negative, CRA owes you a refund)

```

**Zero-rated exports:** SaaS sold to non-Canadian clients (Bennett/US) = zero-rated supply. No HST charged. But ITCs still claimable. This often results in a NET REFUND from CRA — you collect $0 HST but claim back all the HST you paid on expenses.

### Crypto ACB Tracking

**Per-transaction tracking is mandatory.** CRA uses the weighted-average ACB method.

**Tools:**
1. **Koinly** ($49-$279/year) — connects to Kraken, Wealthsimple, wallets; auto-calculates ACB
2. **CoinTracker** ($59-$199/year) — similar to Koinly, slightly better UX
3. **Manual spreadsheet** — free but error-prone; only viable for <50 trades/year

**Spreadsheet columns (if manual):**

| Date | Exchange | Type | Coin | Qty | Price/Unit (CAD) | Total Cost (CAD) | ACB Before | New ACB | Gain/Loss |
|------|----------|------|------|-----|------------------|------------------|------------|---------|-----------|

**Critical rules:**
- Every disposition (sell, swap, spend, gift) is a taxable event
- Transferring between wallets is NOT a taxable event (but record it for ACB continuity)
- Superficial loss rule: cannot claim loss if you repurchase same asset within 30 days before/after sale
- Staking rewards: taxable as income at FMV on receipt date (then new ACB = FMV)

### Mileage/Expense Tracking

**App:** Use MileIQ (free tier: 40 trips/month) or manual log.

**CRA requires:**
- Date of trip
- Destination and business purpose
- Kilometers driven
- Total annual km (business + personal)
- Business-use percentage = business km / total km

**Log template (if manual):**

| Date | From | To | Purpose | KM | Business? |
|------|------|----|---------|----|-----------|

**CRA prescribed rate (2026):** ~$0.72/km first 5,000 km, ~$0.66 after. Use for simplified method.
**Actual cost method:** Track gas, insurance, maintenance, lease/finance payments, parking. Apply business-use %.

### Salary vs. Dividend Decision

**Payroll automation only matters if paying salary.** Post-incorporation, Atlas runs the salary vs. dividend optimization annually:

| Factor | Salary | Dividend |
|--------|--------|---------|
| CPP contributions | Yes (creates RRSP room + CPP pension) | No |
| RRSP room | Yes (18% of salary) | No |
| Corporate tax | Deductible (reduces corporate income) | Not deductible (paid from after-tax profits) |
| Personal tax | Employment income rates | Dividend gross-up + credit |
| Childcare deduction | Creates "earned income" | Does not |
| RRSP room needed? | Pay salary | Pay dividends |

**Atlas default recommendation for early-stage CCPC:** Pay enough salary to max RRSP room (~$175K salary for max $31,560 RRSP room in 2026), take remainder as eligible dividends. Adjust annually based on tax bracket optimization.

# 8. Penalty Avoidance Matrix

> Every common CRA penalty, how to avoid it, and when Atlas should alert CC.

---

| # | Penalty | Trigger | Amount | How to Avoid | If You Get Hit | Atlas Alert |
|---|---------|---------|--------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Late T1 filing** | Filing after June 15 (self-employed) with balance owing | 5% of balance + 1%/month (max 12 months) | File by June 15. Pay by April 30. Even if return incomplete, pay estimated amount. | File immediately. Request penalty relief (RC4288) citing first-time occurrence or extraordinary circumstances. | 30 days before deadline |
| 2 | **Late T1 payment** | Not paying balance by April 30 (even if self-employed) | Daily compound interest at prescribed rate (~6% annually) | Calculate estimated tax and pay by April 30, even before filing. Overpay slightly rather than underpay. | Pay immediately to stop interest accruing. Interest is NOT waived under Taxpayer Relief. | 30 days before deadline |
| 3 | **Late installments** | Missing Mar 15 / Jun 15 / Sep 15 / Dec 15 deadlines | Interest on deficiency (prescribed rate ~6%) + possible surcharge if interest > $1,000 and >25% of required amount | Pay by due dates. Use prior-year method as safe harbor. | Pay ASAP. Consider using current-year method if it results in lower total. Surcharge only applies if negligent. | 21 days before each deadline |
| 4 | **Late T2 filing** | Corporate return filed after 6-month deadline | 5% of balance + 1%/month (max 12 months) | Calendar the deadline. CPA should have it in their system. File extension not available — just file on time. | File immediately. Taxpayer Relief (s.220(3.1)) may waive if extraordinary circumstances. | 45 days before deadline |
| 5 | **Late payroll remittance** | Not remitting CPP/EI/tax by 15th of following month | 3% (1-3 days late), 5% (4-5 days), 7% (6-7 days), 10% (8+ days or repeated). Interest compounds daily. | Auto-remit via payroll software. Never manually remit. | Pay immediately. Penalty applies per occurrence. Repeated late = 20% penalty. | 10 days before each 15th |
| 6 | **Late HST remittance** | Not remitting net HST by filing deadline | 1% of amount (≤7 days), 25%/yr interest rate, penalty up to 4% | Calendar filing dates. Auto-pay via CRA My Business Account. Keep HST in segregated account. | Pay immediately. Apply for penalty relief if first occurrence. | 21 days before deadline |
| 7 | **Late T4/T4A filing** | Not filing information returns by Feb 28 | $100/day, minimum $100, maximum $7,500 per filing | Calendar Feb 28. Payroll software auto-generates. | File immediately. Penalty accrues daily. | 21 days before Feb 28 |
| 8 | **Late T1135** | Not filing foreign property report with T1/T2 | $25/day, max $2,500. Gross negligence: $1,000/month, max $24,000 | Monitor foreign property cost quarterly. File with tax return. | File immediately via VDP if >1 year late (penalty elimination). Otherwise file late and eat the $25/day. | Quarterly monitoring |
| 9 | **TFSA over-contribution** | Contributing more than available room | 1%/month on excess amount, every month it remains | Track room precisely. CRA My Account shows room. When in doubt, contribute less. | Withdraw excess immediately. File RC243 form. CRA may waive if reasonable error. | Before each contribution |
| 10 | **FHSA over-contribution** | Contributing more than $8K/year or $40K lifetime | 1%/month on excess amount | Track contributions. $8K annual limit is firm. Unused room carries forward (max $8K). | Withdraw excess immediately. Apply for waiver. | Before each contribution |
| 11 | **RRSP over-contribution** | Contributing more than deduction limit + $2,000 buffer | 1%/month on excess above $2,000 buffer | Check NOA for deduction limit. CRA My Account shows limit. | Withdraw excess (T3012A form). $2,000 lifetime buffer = free cushion. | Before contributions |
| 12 | **HST non-registration** | Failing to register when taxable supplies > $30K | Assessed for HST that SHOULD have been collected (13% of revenue). Cannot claim ITCs for unregistered period. | Monitor rolling 4-quarter revenue. Register proactively before crossing threshold. | Register immediately. Consider VDP. May recover partial ITCs retroactively (4 years). | When revenue reaches $25K (83% of threshold) |
| 13 | **Repeated late filing** | Filing T1 late more than once in 3-year period | Penalty doubles: 10% of balance + 2%/month (max 20 months) | Never file late. If you cannot file on time, file anyway with NIL return and amend later. | Apply for Taxpayer Relief. Document any extraordinary circumstances. | Extra urgency |
| 14 | **Gross negligence** | False statements or omissions on return (knowing or careless) | 50% of tax on understated income (s.163(2)) | Accurate reporting. Document all positions. Professional advice for aggressive positions. | Immediately engage a tax lawyer (NOT accountant — privilege applies). Object to assessment within 90 days. | N/A (prevention only) |
| 15 | **Missing receipts** | No documentation for claimed expenses | Denied deductions = higher tax + possible penalty | Receipt capture workflow. 6-year retention minimum. Digital copies acceptable. | Reconstruct documentation: bank statements, credit card records, vendor invoices. CRA accepts substitutes if reasonable effort shown. | Monthly receipt check |
