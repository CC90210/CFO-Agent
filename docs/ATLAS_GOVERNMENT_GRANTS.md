### 1.1 Canada Small Business Financing Program (CSBFP)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Type** | Government-guaranteed loan |
| **Amount** | Up to **$1,000,000** total ($350K for equipment/leasehold, $150K for intangible assets/working capital) |
| **Who qualifies** | Canadian businesses with gross annual revenue under $10M |
| **Interest rate** | Prime + 3% (variable) or lender's residential mortgage rate + 3% (fixed) |
| **Registration fee** | 2% of loan amount (can be financed into the loan) |
| **Term** | Up to 15 years (real property), 10 years (equipment), 5 years (intangible assets/working capital) |
| **Government guarantee** | 85% — lenders love this because their risk is capped |

**Why CC should care:** This is the easiest large loan to get in Canada. Banks cannot refuse you based on business age alone — the government backs 85% of the loan. Use it for equipment (computers, servers), leasehold improvements (if CC gets office space), or working capital.

**What it covers:**
- Equipment and technology purchases (servers, GPUs, workstations)
- Leasehold improvements
- Intangible assets and working capital (new as of 2022 reform)
- Software licenses (if capitalized)

**What it does NOT cover:**
- Inventory, franchise fees, goodwill, R&D costs, working capital above $150K

**CC's play:** Apply through RBC (existing relationship) for $50K-$150K for equipment + working capital. The 85% government guarantee makes this nearly risk-free for the bank.

**Apply:** Through any chartered bank, credit union, or caisse populaire. Ask for "CSBFP loan" specifically — many loan officers will try to steer you to conventional products.

**Link:** [ic.gc.ca/eic/site/csbfp-pfpec.nsf/eng/home](https://ic.gc.ca/eic/site/csbfp-pfpec.nsf/eng/home)

### 1.5 SR&ED Tax Credits (Scientific Research & Experimental Development)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Type** | Tax credit (refundable for CCPCs) |
| **Federal rate** | **35% refundable** on first $3M of qualified SR&ED expenditures (for CCPCs with < $800K taxable income) |
| **Sole proprietor rate** | **15% non-refundable** (federal) — CC gets the lower rate as a sole prop |
| **Ontario OITC** | Additional **8% non-refundable** on Ontario SR&ED |
| **Combined (sole prop)** | ~23% non-refundable credit on eligible R&D expenses |
| **Combined (CCPC)** | ~43% refundable — this is why incorporation matters for R&D companies |

**What qualifies as SR&ED for OASIS AI Solutions:**
- Developing novel AI automation workflows that involve technological uncertainty
- Building custom NLP/ML models (not just using APIs — must demonstrate advancement)
- Systematic investigation: hypothesis, testing, iteration, documentation
- Training custom models, fine-tuning, developing novel prompts with systematic experimentation

**What does NOT qualify:**
- Using off-the-shelf AI APIs without modification (just calling OpenAI/Claude API = no)
- Market research, quality control, routine data collection
- Style changes, cosmetic improvements
- Social science or humanities research

**How to file SR&ED:**
1. **Document as you go** — Keep a project log: what was the technological uncertainty? What did you try? What worked/failed? Date everything.
2. **File with T1 return** — Form T661 (SR&ED claim) filed with your annual tax return
3. **Timeline:** Must be filed within **12 months** of the tax filing deadline for that year
4. **CRA review:** 60-120 days for routine claims. Financial review first, then technical review if flagged.
5. **First-time claimant program:** CRA offers pre-claim consultations for first-time filers — USE THIS.

**SR&ED Consultants:**
- Typically charge 15-25% of the credit received (contingency basis — you pay nothing if the claim fails)
- Top firms: Northbridge Consultants, SRED.ca, G6 Consulting, Ryan (formerly Catax)
- For a sole prop with $50K-$100K in eligible expenses, a consultant is worth it to avoid CRA rejection
- Ask for "contingency fee" — no upfront cost

**CRA Review Timeline:**
- **Weeks 1-8:** Financial review (are expenses real and documented?)
- **Weeks 8-16:** Technical review (does the work qualify as SR&ED?)
- **Common rejection reasons:** Insufficient documentation, no technological uncertainty demonstrated, work is routine engineering not experimental development
- **Appeal:** Notice of Objection within 90 days of reassessment

**CC's play:** If CC incorporates (CCPC), the SR&ED credit jumps from 23% non-refundable to 43% REFUNDABLE. On $100K of eligible R&D expenses, that is $43,000 cash back. This alone justifies incorporation for an AI company doing genuine R&D. Even as sole prop, 23% is significant. Start documenting ALL experimental AI work now.

**Key reference:** See `docs/ATLAS_TAX_STRATEGY.md` for full SR&ED integration with CC's tax plan.

### 2.2 Ontario Interactive Digital Media Tax Credit (OIDMTC)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Type** | Refundable tax credit |
| **Rate** | **35%** of eligible Ontario labour expenditures (40% for small companies with < $20M gross revenue) |
| **Annual cap** | **$3M** in eligible expenditures per year |
| **Max credit** | ~$1.05M-$1.2M per year |

**Does AI SaaS qualify?** This is the critical question.

**Eligible products:**
- Interactive digital media products that educate, inform, or entertain
- Must have 2+ of: text, sound, images, video with user interaction
- Includes: apps, games, educational software, interactive websites with significant functionality
- The product must be offered for sale or license

**Potentially eligible for OASIS AI Solutions:**
- AI-powered interactive dashboards or tools sold to clients
- SaaS platforms with interactive user interfaces
- AI training/educational products
- Client-facing automation tools with interactive elements

**Likely NOT eligible:**
- Backend-only AI services (APIs without interactive frontend)
- Pure consulting/implementation services
- Internal tools not sold to customers

**CC's play:** If any OASIS AI product has an interactive digital media component (dashboard, interactive SaaS tool, educational platform), this is a massive 35-40% credit on labour costs. CC should consult with an OIDMTC specialist to assess eligibility. Even partial eligibility on one product line is worth pursuing.

**Certification:** Apply through Ontario Creates for a certificate of eligibility. Must be obtained BEFORE claiming the credit.

**Apply:** [ontariocreates.ca/tax-incentives/oidmtc](https://www.ontariocreates.ca/tax-incentives/oidmtc)

## SECTION 5: How to Actually Apply

### Top 5 Most Accessible Programs — Step-by-Step

#### PROGRAM 1: Starter Company Plus ($5,000 Grant)

**Difficulty: EASY | Timeline: 6-12 weeks | CC Eligible: YES**

1. **Contact SBEC** — Call the Simcoe County Small Business Enterprise Centre. Ask about the next Starter Company Plus intake.
2. **Register** — Fill out the intake form. Provide basic business info (name, type, stage).
3. **Attend training** — Complete their entrepreneur training program (usually 2-4 sessions over a few weeks). Topics: business planning, financial management, marketing, legal.
4. **Write business plan** — Using their template. SBEC advisors will help. Include: executive summary, market analysis, financial projections (12 months), marketing plan.
5. **Meet with mentor** — Assigned mentor reviews your plan, provides feedback.
6. **Submit application** — Business plan + training completion certificate + financial projections.
7. **Panel review** — Advisory panel reviews and approves/denies.
8. **Receive funds** — Grant deposited. Must be used for business purposes (equipment, marketing, inventory, training).
9. **Reporting** — Brief report on how funds were used (typically at 6 months).

**Common rejection reasons:**
- Incomplete business plan (especially weak financial projections)
- Business is too mature / already well-funded
- Not attending all training sessions
- Unclear value proposition

---

#### PROGRAM 2: Futurpreneur Canada ($60,000 Loan + Mentorship)

**Difficulty: MODERATE | Timeline: 4-8 weeks | CC Eligible: LIKELY**

1. **Online application** — [futurpreneur.ca/en/get-started](https://www.futurpreneur.ca/en/get-started/). Basic info, business concept, personal financial overview.
2. **Caseworker assignment** — Futurpreneur assigns a caseworker who guides you through the process.
3. **Business plan development** — Using Futurpreneur's business plan writer tool. Must include: 2-year cash flow projections, market analysis, competitive landscape, pricing strategy.
4. **Personal credit check** — They pull your credit. No bankruptcies in last 7 years. Reasonable credit score needed (not perfect).
5. **Caseworker review** — Your caseworker reviews the plan, asks for revisions.
6. **Loan committee** — Regional loan committee reviews and approves.
7. **BDC co-lending** — If requesting the additional $40K from BDC, separate BDC review.
8. **Mentor matching** — Matched with a business mentor for 2 years (mandatory).
9. **Disbursement** — Funds released. Typically within 1-2 weeks of approval.
10. **Repayment** — Monthly payments over up to 5 years. Grace period may be available.

**Common rejection reasons:**
- Business is too old (>12 months operating) — discuss with caseworker
- Poor personal credit
- Unrealistic financial projections
- No clear path to repayment
- Franchise (not eligible)

---

#### PROGRAM 3: CSBFP Loan (up to $1,000,000)

**Difficulty: MODERATE | Timeline: 2-6 weeks | CC Eligible: YES**

1. **Choose a lender** — Go to RBC (CC's existing bank). Ask specifically for a "Canada Small Business Financing Program loan."
2. **Prepare documentation:**
   - Business plan (can be simpler than Futurpreneur's)
   - Financial projections (12-24 months)
   - Purpose of loan (equipment list, lease agreement, etc.)
   - Personal financial statement
   - Proof of business registration
3. **Submit application** — Through the bank. The bank applies to ISED for the government guarantee.
4. **Bank assessment** — Bank does their own credit assessment. They are more lenient because of the 85% government guarantee.
5. **Registration** — Bank registers the loan with ISED. 2% registration fee charged (can be rolled into loan).
6. **Disbursement** — Loan funds available. Use only for approved purposes.
7. **Repayment** — Monthly payments over the loan term.

**Common rejection reasons:**
- Loan officer doesn't know about CSBFP (ask specifically by name)
- Business revenue < $10M check (easy for CC)
- Poor personal credit
- Loan purpose doesn't fit eligible categories (R&D not eligible — use for equipment instead)
- Bank tries to offer conventional product instead (insist on CSBFP)

**Pro tip:** If RBC says no, try a credit union. They often have higher CSBFP approval rates.

#### PROGRAM 4: Cloud Credits ($100K-$200K)

**Difficulty: EASY | Timeline: 1-2 weeks | CC Eligible: YES**

1. **AWS Activate:**
   - Go to [aws.amazon.com/activate](https://aws.amazon.com/activate/)
   - Apply as a self-funded startup (no VC required)
   - Provide: company website, description, founding date, team size
   - Approval typically within days
   - Credits activated on your AWS account

2. **Google for Startups Cloud Program:**
   - Go to [cloud.google.com/startup](https://cloud.google.com/startup)
   - Apply directly or through a partner (accelerator, VC, etc.)
   - Provide: company info, product description, cloud usage plans
   - Higher credit amounts if applied through a partner

3. **Microsoft for Startups Founders Hub:**
   - Go to [startups.microsoft.com](https://startups.microsoft.com/)
   - No third-party validation needed
   - Also includes GitHub Enterprise, LinkedIn Learning, Microsoft 365
   - Fastest approval of the three

**Cannot be rejected if:** You have a real business, a working website, and a product that uses cloud services.

#### PROGRAM 5: CDL (Creative Destruction Lab) — Equity-Free AI Accelerator

**Difficulty: COMPETITIVE | Timeline: 2-3 months (application to acceptance) | CC Eligible: YES**

1. **Check application window** — CDL typically opens applications in spring/summer for fall cohort. Check [creativedestructionlab.com](https://www.creativedestructionlab.com/).
2. **Prepare application:**
   - Describe the technical innovation (what is novel about OASIS AI?)
   - Market opportunity ($TAM, $SAM, $SOM)
   - Team (CC's background, skills, vision)
   - Traction (clients, revenue, pilots)
   - What you would gain from CDL
3. **Submit application** — Online portal.
4. **Interview** — If shortlisted, video interview with CDL team.
5. **Acceptance** — Notified of acceptance into the cohort.
6. **Program** — 9-month program with objective-setting sessions every 8 weeks. Meet with business leaders and scientists who act as mentors.
7. **No equity taken** — CDL does not take equity. Pure mentorship + network.

**Common rejection reasons:**
- Not enough technical novelty (just using APIs is not enough)
- Too early stage (need some traction or a working prototype)
- Not demonstrating a scalable business model
- Weak application essays

### Stacking Grants — Which Programs Can Be Combined

**YES, stack these:**
- Starter Company Plus ($5K) + Futurpreneur ($60K) + CSBFP ($350K+) + Cloud Credits ($200K+)
- SR&ED federal (15%) + OITC provincial (8%) = 23% combined tax credit
- Canada Job Grant + Canada Summer Jobs (for different employees or different periods)
- IRAP funding + SR&ED credits (IRAP-funded work can also be claimed for SR&ED, but IRAP reduces the SR&ED eligible base)
- CanExport + any domestic grant (different purposes — export vs domestic development)

**CANNOT stack:**
- Futurpreneur + BDC standalone loan (Futurpreneur already includes BDC co-lending)
- Multiple cloud provider credits ARE stackable (use AWS for compute, GCP for AI/ML, Azure for enterprise services)
- OIDMTC + SR&ED on the same labour expenditures (must choose one credit per expenditure, but can split across different project activities)
