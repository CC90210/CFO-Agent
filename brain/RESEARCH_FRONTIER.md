## IN-STUDY — 2026-04-19 cycle

### Hyman Minsky — Financial Instability Hypothesis
**Primary:** *Stabilizing an Unstable Economy* (1986), *Can "It" Happen Again?* (1982)
**Why now:** 2026 macro has elements of late-cycle credit (ZIRP-era debt maturing into higher rates). Minsky's hedge → speculative → Ponzi finance progression is the cleanest frame for where a given sector sits in the cycle.
**Application target:** macro layer of [[skills/financial-planning/SKILL|financial-planning]] + stock research bear case. If we can classify the target's funding model (hedge vs speculative vs Ponzi), we have a tail-risk read independent of current price action.
**Status gate:** first application → APPLIED on a real research pick.

### Andrew Lo — Adaptive Markets Hypothesis
**Primary:** *Adaptive Markets* (2017), Lo's MIT papers on behavioral + evolutionary market modeling
**Why now:** Resolves the perpetual Buffett-vs-EMH argument cleanly. Markets are neither efficient nor inefficient — they adapt. Behavioral anomalies erode as more participants learn them.
**Application target:** a second-level thinking upgrade. Per Marks, first-level sees the price; second-level sees the consensus. Lo adds: third-level sees which participants have already adapted to the consensus and which haven't. Useful for retail-vs-institutional sentiment divergence calls.
**Status gate:** one stock pick where we explicitly name the "who hasn't adapted yet" thesis component.

### Perry Mehrling — The Money View
**Primary:** *The New Lombard Street* (2011), MIT/INET lectures, papers on dealer-based payments
**Why now:** Crypto + stablecoins + BRICS payment systems + CBDCs — the plumbing layer of finance is being rebuilt. Most commentary is surface-level. Mehrling's dealer/payments framework is the best lens for understanding what actually happens when money moves.
**Application target:** when CC asks about crypto regulation, stablecoin allocation, or cross-border USD access for OASIS clients — Mehrling's dealer-hierarchy frame answers cleanly.
**Status gate:** one cross-border payment-structure question answered with this frame.

### Reinhart-Rogoff — This Time Is Different
**Primary:** *This Time Is Different: Eight Centuries of Financial Folly* (2009), *From Financial Crash to Debt Crisis* (2014)
**Why now:** Sovereign risk is back in conversation (US debt trajectory, EU peripherals, EM defaults). CC's exit ladder touches multiple jurisdictions — sovereign-risk awareness is the tax-lawyer's complement to the tax-code-lawyer's knowledge.
**Application target:** [[skills/departure-tax-planning/SKILL|departure-tax-planning]] + [[skills/cross-border-compliance/SKILL|cross-border-compliance]] get a "sovereign risk level" input. Higher sovereign risk in destination jurisdiction = lower advisable asset concentration there.
**Status gate:** one Crown Dependencies cost-benefit analysis with sovereign-risk scoring.

### William N. Goetzmann — Money Changes Everything
**Primary:** *Money Changes Everything: How Finance Made Civilization Possible* (2016), his Yale SOM course
**Why now:** Foundational reading. Not decision-urgent. But: every time Atlas needs to explain "why does X tax rule exist" or "why is this financial instrument shaped this way," the historical answer shortens the explanation by 10x.
**Application target:** [[INTERACTION_PROTOCOL]] teaching moments — when CC asks "why is this the way it is?" Goetzmann's historical framing beats abstract theory every time.
**Status gate:** no hard gate; background reading. Will promote individual chapters as they apply.

## Integration rules

1. **No entry moves to APPLIED without a named real decision.** No "I could see using this" — just "I used this on 2026-05-XX to decide Y."
2. **Promotion to CFO_CANON requires 3 APPLIED uses** with outcomes Atlas would repeat. Matches Protocol 3 of [[skills/self-improvement-protocol/SKILL|self-improvement-protocol]].
3. **Drops are OK** — if a framework doesn't earn its keep, remove it. Goetzmann-style foundational reading is the exception (always-useful context, never time-critical).
4. **Sources must be primary.** Secondary commentary is fine for orientation; don't promote on secondary-source strength.
5. **Link every entry to its target application.** An entry without an application target is a collection-hobbyist's index card, not research.
